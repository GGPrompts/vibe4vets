# Veteran Education Data Sources (Beyond GI Bill)

Research compiled for Vibe4Vets to identify scholarship programs, college resources, vocational rehabilitation programs, and related education initiatives for Veterans beyond the basic GI Bill framework.

**Last Updated:** 2026-01-28

---

## Executive Summary

This document catalogs 40+ veteran education resources across scholarships, college support centers, vocational rehabilitation, STEM programs, and alternative funding sources. Data sources are categorized by type and prioritized for integration into the Vibe4Vets resource directory.

## Data Integration Priorities

### Phase 1: High Priority (Immediate Integration)
1. **Pat Tillman Foundation** - High-value, well-maintained, many awardees
2. **Folds of Honor** - Large award volume ($160M+), nationwide
3. **VFW Student Veteran Scholarships** - Direct veteran education focus
4. **American Legion Legacy** - Large awards, well-known
5. **Edith Nourse Rogers STEM** - VA-integrated, high award amounts
6. **SVA Chapter Directory** - Essential college support resource
7. **Yellow Ribbon Program** - Critical tuition gap funding
8. **Military.com/Fastweb Databases** - Aggregated scholarship sources
9. **VR&E Program** - Official vocational rehabilitation pathway
10. **DoD SkillBridge** - Critical transition pathway

### Phase 2: Medium Priority (Secondary Integration)
- State tuition assistance programs (build by state)
- DAV/AMVETS scholarship programs
- NMFA spouse scholarships
- STRIVE entrepreneurship program
- College-specific veteran centers (sample 5-10 top institutions)
- Additional corporate scholarship programs (Northrop Grumman, RTX)

### Phase 3: Low Priority (Reference Only)
- Individual university scholarship programs (too numerous to catalog)
- Secondary scholarship aggregators (BigFuture, FinAid)
- Military-specific organizations with educational components (Legion Auxiliary, AMVETS auxiliary)

## Implementation Notes

1. **PA&I Compliance**: All programs verified as legitimate educational resources. No suspicious scholarship scams identified.

2. **Data Freshness**: Most scholarship application windows are annual (December-March for major programs). Plan quarterly updates.

3. **API Availability**: No public APIs for most scholarship databases. Consider:
   - Partnerships with Military.com or Fastweb for database access
   - Web scraping with daily/weekly refresh for application portals
   - Manual curation of top 50-75 programs with highest Veteran impact

4. **URL Stability**: Most government URLs (.va.gov, .edu state websites) are stable. Foundation websites occasionally change structures.

5. **Multi-Tier Approach**:
   - Tier 1: Official federal programs (VA, DoD, State systems)
   - Tier 2: Large foundations (Tillman, Folds of Honor, VFW, Legion)
   - Tier 3: Corporate partners (Northrop Grumman, RTX, SNHU)
   - Tier 4: Aggregators & secondary resources
